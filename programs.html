The Player's Pathway

We don't mix beginners with pros. You train with peers at your exact skill level to ensure optimal growth.

1
Foundation Phase

Beginners

Focus is purely on motor skills, hand-eye coordination, rules of the game, and having fun. No pressure to play competitive matches.

2
Development Phase

Intermediates

Transitioning from fun games into serious technical drills. Players start taking part in internal friendlies and learn tactical set-pieces.

3
Performance Phase

Advanced / Pro

High-intensity, physically demanding training. Strict diets, video form analysis, and weekly competitive state/district tournament entries.

Age Breakdown

We group athletes strictly by biological age and physical development to prevent injuries.

  • U-10 (6-9 Yrs): Foundation and basic rules. Focus on agility.
  • U-14 (10-13 Yrs): Technical skill acquisition and stamina building.
  • U-19 (14-18 Yrs): Strength conditioning and tactical play.
  • Seniors (19+ Yrs): High performance and tournament readiness.

Safety First Methodology

A sports career can be ruined by a single untended injury. We take athletic safety extremely seriously.

Medical Facilities

On-site level 2 trauma kit, ice baths, and a full-time certified sports physiotherapist.

Equipment Safety

Mandatory ISI/CE marked helmets and protective gear enforcement during all net sessions and matches.

Recovery Protocols

Strict cool-down routines post-training and mandatory rest days to prevent muscle burnout and stress fractures.
